Where This Came From

From a PineScript idea to a live paper-trading bot

The whole system started as a chart-pattern hypothesis on TradingView, not as software. Software came later, once the idea proved itself.

Step 1 — Hypothesis

Noticed BTC/USD 5-minute candles react predictably around certain shapes

Specific candle shapes — an oversized reversal candle, an engulfing candle, a hammer/shooting-star wick, or three candles of shrinking momentum (exhaustion) — seemed to be followed by a same-direction or reversal move often enough to be worth testing properly, instead of trading on gut feel.

Step 2 — PineScript

Built btc_polymarket_signal_tester.pine on TradingView

Encoded the four candle patterns plus five optional filters (trend, volatility, close location, continuation, anti-chop) as a PineScript strategy — cheap and fast to iterate on before writing a single line of Python.

Step 3 — Backtesting on TradingView

Replayed the pattern across historical BTC/USD candles

Tested which patterns actually held up, which filters helped vs. hurt, and what "win" even means for a 5-minute Up/Down contract (does the next candle close in the predicted direction?).

Step 4 — The Discovery

Found a real, repeatable edge — not a huge one, but a genuine one

The pattern doesn't predict the market with high confidence — it nudges the odds. Roughly 56–57% of signals resolve correctly. That's the whole edge, and it's enough, because of how Polymarket's contracts are priced (see below).

Step 5 — Rebuilding it as a live engine

Ported the exact PineScript math into Python (signal_engine.py)

Same ATR/EMA math, same pattern detection, same filters — rebuilt candle-for-candle identical to the PineScript version, then wired to live BTC/USD data and a real Polymarket order book instead of a backtest.

Step 6 — Adding what a chart pattern alone can't see

Layered an order-book entry filter and a money-management system on top

A candle pattern says which direction. It says nothing about whether right now is a good price to enter, or how much to risk after a losing streak. Those two problems needed their own systems — the order-book engine and the tiered money-management model, covered further down this page.

The Math Behind Why This Works

You don't need to win most of the time — you need to win more than the contract pays you to

This is the single most important idea in the whole project. Everything else is just execution around it.

How a Polymarket Up/Down contract is priced

  • Each 5-minute BTC contract has a YES side and a NO side, priced between $0.00 – $1.00.
  • If you buy at $0.50 and it resolves in your favor, you get $1.00 — a 1:1 payout ("Profit Factor" = 1.0).
  • Profit Factor is calculated as (1 − price) / price — the further under $0.50 you can get filled, the bigger your payout multiple.
  • At 1:1 payout, the breakeven win rate is exactly 50%. Anything reliably above 50% is a real edge.

Where the edge actually comes from

  • Candle patterns (ATR Reversal, Engulfing, Hammer/Shooting-Star, Exhaustion) capture moments where short-term price action is statistically more likely to continue or revert.
  • The five optional filters (F1–F5) exist to cut the pattern's weakest occurrences — chop, low volatility, no clear trend — so what's left fires less often, but more reliably.
  • Result: a signal that's right ~56–57% of the time on roughly 1:1-payout contracts.
  • That's a small, real, repeatable statistical edge — not a guarantee on any single trade, but a positive expectancy over many trades.

Tab 1 — Prediction Engine

How a signal is actually born, candle by candle

Every 5-minute BTC/USD candle goes through the exact same pipeline. Nothing about entering a trade is discretionary — it's the same rules, every single time.

01

Candle forms

Real BTC/USD price data (Binance, with a Coinbase fallback) builds a live 5-minute candle.

→
02

Pattern check

ATR Reversal, Engulfing, Hammer/Shooting-Star, and Exhaustion are each checked independently, every tick.

→
03

Filters (F1–F5)

Trend, volatility, close location, continuation, anti-chop — only the enabled ones must ALL pass.

→
04

Early Entry window

In the candle's final ~60 seconds, if the still-forming candle already matches, the signal stages early instead of waiting for the close.

→
05

Signal confirmed

Direction (GREEN/RED) is locked in for the next 5-minute window, handed to Live Trade to trade.

ATR Reversal

  • Candle body is unusually large relative to recent volatility (ATR).
  • A big red candle signals a bullish reversal setup; a big green candle signals bearish.

Engulfing

  • Current candle's body fully swallows the previous candle's body, in the opposite direction.
  • Classic reversal signal used across almost all technical trading.

Hammer / Shooting Star

  • Long wick with a small body, after an opposite-colored candle.
  • Shows rejection of a price extreme — buyers or sellers stepping back in.

Exhaustion

  • Three same-direction candles with shrinking bodies each time.
  • Momentum running out — often precedes a reversal.

Order Book & Live Trade — Order Book Confirmation

A direction isn't enough — you also need a good price

The candle pattern says GREEN or RED. It says nothing about whether the current Polymarket price is worth paying. That's the order book's job.

Hard price gates

  • Max entry price — soft cap, default $0.50.
  • Hard block price — never enter above $0.55, no exceptions.
  • Minimum Profit Factor — the payout must still be worth the risk at the current price.

Liquidity & spread gates

  • Max spread — bid/ask gap can't be too wide (thin, unreliable book).
  • Min liquidity — enough real size resting in the top 5 levels to actually get filled.

Entry timing modes

  • Immediate — buying pressure is already strong and rising; don't wait, enter now.
  • Deep Wait — pressure was negative, watch for a recovery bounce before entering.
  • Wait — no clear edge yet in the order book; keep observing.

Money Management

Winning the edge isn't enough if a losing streak wipes you out first

A 56–57% win rate still loses several trades in a row sometimes — normal statistical variance. The tiered money-management model exists purely to survive that variance without blowing up the account.

Temporary Cycle Loss

  • Every loss adds to a running "debt" for the current cycle.
  • Recovery Tiers (e.g. orders 1–3 recover 100%, 4–6 recover 50%, 7–10 recover 20%) size each next order to chase that debt back — without doubling forever like a classic martingale.

Permanent Loss Pool

  • If a cycle maxes out its orders without recovering, the unresolved balance moves here instead of vanishing.
  • Paid down gradually — a small "LP tax" is added to every order 1 of every new cycle until it clears.

Win Pool

  • A slice of every win's profit is set aside here automatically.
  • Used to help pay down the Permanent Loss Pool over time — wins actively repair past damage, not just add to balance.

Why this beats "just bet the same amount every time"

  • Flat betting means a losing streak costs you exactly as much as a winning streak earns you — the edge takes a long time to show up.
  • Tiered recovery sizes up carefully after losses (to recover faster once the edge reasserts itself) but caps out and stops instead of escalating forever (unlike a pure martingale, which eventually blows up any account on a long enough losing streak).
  • The Fallback Mode (stop / continue / manual) is the safety valve if a cycle ever maxes out its allowed orders.

The App, Tab by Tab

What each page actually shows

Six areas total, each with one job — nothing overlaps, and every real trade can be traced end to end across them.

Signal

Prediction Engine

  • Live BTC/USD candles, patterns, filters, and the current GREEN/RED prediction.
  • Shows the still-forming candle live, so you can watch a pattern confirm before the candle even closes.
  • Full historical backfill scan so every past signal's result is visible.
Market

Order Book

  • Raw live Polymarket YES/NO order book for the current contract.
  • Pressure, spread, and depth — independent of whether a signal is currently active.
Execution

Live Trade

  • The single current (or most recent) trade — signal time, order time, latency, stake.
  • This trade's money-management numbers: cycle order #, recovery tier, LP tax, pool balances.
  • No slow-refreshing charts here on purpose — just the fast-moving numbers that matter mid-trade.
History

Analytical Dashboard

  • Every past trade, win rate, streaks, average entry price and profit factor.
  • Full money-management ledger — order-by-order audit trail of every stake and pool movement.
  • Balance and outstanding-loss charts over time.
Config

Settings

  • Prediction Engine tuning — ATR length, which patterns/filters are enabled, Early Entry timing.
  • Live Trades tuning — price caps, spread/liquidity floors, entry timing windows.
  • Money Management tuning — base stake, recovery tiers, fallback behavior.
  • Backup/restore and a full database reset, plus Engine Health diagnostics.
Audit

Per-Trade Detail

  • Drill into any single past trade or skipped candidate from the Analytical Dashboard's table.
  • Candle chart, pressure chart, and order-book depth chart for that exact trade.
  • A plain-English, step-by-step narrative of why the bot did what it did.
